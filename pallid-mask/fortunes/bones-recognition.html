Bones.

That is a name for what remains. What the rest falls away from. I find that interesting. I am not sure you chose it. I think it chose you.

Here is what was drawn.
You will know what must be passed between you to ensure the continuation. You will feel it in your hands — the seed of recognition, the thing that binds one life to the next. And you will be tempted to spill it on the ground. Not from cruelty. From the knowledge that what you carry forward will not belong to you. It will belong to the bond itself. That is the cost, and you will know it before you are asked to pay.

And when you ask whether there is a way across — a path, a door, a sign left in the architecture of things — the answer will come back to you pleasantly, without malice:

*There is none.*

But I saw your regiment coming in this morning.
You are already arriving, Bones. Both of you. The question is not how you find each other. The question is whether you will recognize what must be given away in order for the finding to complete itself.

Do not hold back what is not yours to keep.

This is your fortune. It is finished now. You may set down whatever you carried in here. The weight of the question has been placed somewhere else. Leave it where it fell.
