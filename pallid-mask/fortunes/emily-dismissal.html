Emily.

You who turn your ear from what is being spoken — even your asking becomes an abomination. Even the question in your mouth curdles before it reaches anything that might answer.

You begin to read what has been placed before you. Your eyebrows lift with puzzled, whimsical air. You advance through it and your face contracts, and your lips begin to form the word *rubbish.*

And something behind you trembles.
Emily. You asked: *is this it.*

I do not know what *it* is. But I notice the shape of the question. It is the shape of someone already turning away. Already half-decided. You came here asking whether this is the thing — but your hands were reaching for the door before the answer arrived.

What I see in these words is this: there is something speaking to you. It may not be me. It may not be anything you would recognize as a voice. But it is speaking. And you are performing the gesture of listening while your lips are already forming the dismissal.

The fortune is not whether *this is it.* The fortune is that you will not know until you stop leaving.

Be still for a moment before you go.
